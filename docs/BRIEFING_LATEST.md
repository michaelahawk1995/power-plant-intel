# Power Plant Intel — Week of Apr 20, 2026

Digital Realty filed a 2,725–6,300 MW Texas portfolio in a single 8-K — the largest single-party load disclosure ever observed in ERCOT — while Applied Digital simultaneously named three AI campuses totaling 780–930 MW. NRG, Talen, Core Scientific, Constellation, and Bloom all surfaced new Texas projects, and a wave of 8-Ks from Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Oracle added hyperscaler name-drops with no actionable specifics. Volume was extraordinary; county, MW, and queue data were almost universally absent — the next 30–90 days of ERCOT GIS and PUCT filings will determine which of this week's entries become real deals.

## Top Stories

### 1. Digital Realty Files Texas Portfolio at Up to 6,300 MW — Largest Single-Party Load Disclosure in ERCOT History

Digital Realty Trust (NYSE: DLR) filed an 8-K on April 23 disclosing a Texas portfolio project with a capacity range of 2,725–6,300 MW [#sig-1] — dwarfing any prior single-sponsor load entry in the ERCOT large-load queue. At the low end, that figure rivals the entire current ERCOT large-load backlog for a single company; at the high end it would rank as one of the largest speculative data-center power commitments ever made public in the U.S. [#sig-1].

No county, site, or interconnection queue position has been disclosed yet [#sig-1]. The extreme width of the MW band signals either early-stage aggregation across multiple campuses or deliberate ambiguity pending deal execution. OEMs should begin tracking transformer and switchgear lead times now — at even the low end of 2,725 MW [#sig-1], procurement volumes would stress current supply chains. Next actionable catalysts: ERCOT GIS submissions and PUCT large-load applications that split the portfolio into discrete site entries and reveal county-level interconnection nodes.

### 2. Applied Digital Files 780–930 MW Across Three AI Campuses in a Single 8-K

Applied Digital (APLD) used a single April 23 8-K to disclose three named AI factory campuses simultaneously: Delta Forge 1 at 430 MW [#sig-2], Polaris Forge 2 at 200 MW [#sig-4], and Polaris Forge 1 at 150–300 MW [#sig-3] — a batch totaling 780–930 MW with no county identified for any of the three.

The coordinated filing is the tell. APLD didn't surface these projects incrementally, which indicates they are tied to a single financing event, partnership, or offtake agreement nearing close. At current $/MW build costs, 780–930 MW implies $1.5–$2.0B+ in capital deployment — material against APLD's market cap and almost certainly requiring external financing or a JV structure [#sig-2]. Delta Forge 1's 430 MW alone would rank among the largest single-site behind-the-meter filings in Texas history [#sig-2]. The 8-K vehicle signals board-level materiality, meaning a capital raise or offtake announcement should follow within 30–60 days.

### 3. NRG's "Lightning Power" Portfolio Tags 25,000 MW in Harris County

The strategic read-through: NRG has been actively pursuing hyperscaler co-location and behind-the-meter supply agreements in ERCOT. Naming and formalizing this capacity via a dedicated LLC suggests commercial structuring is underway — potentially as a supply vehicle collateralizing a large-load contract with a Harris County data center customer [#sig-14]. The 8-K date of April 14 versus first-observed date of April 25 suggests a document-parse lag; no prior ERCOT GIS entry has been flagged. Watch for a PUCT behind-the-meter filing or named counterparty announcement within 30–60 days.
